Mapping Archives: Mohawk Land Dispossession by Sulpician Priests
Visualize how contractually promised land was taken from the Mohawk of Kanehsatà:ke over the course of 200 years.
This map was made as a testimony to the Two Dog Wampum. Wampum are chains of friendship binding groups around specific responsibilities and obligations. They need to be regularly polished to renew and confirm commitments and keep communications channels open. This is a process used by the this is a process used by the Mohawk of Kanehsatà:ke (hearafter the Kanehsata'kehró:non) to preserve peace with other nations, including the British Crown and the Seminary of St Sulpice. Although the Kanehsata'keró:non repeatedly delivered this Two Dog Wampum to British officials to honour their promise and protect the land from white settlement, the British upheld the claim of the Sulpician priests. The fact that the people of Kanehsatà: ke were never recognized as the rightful title holders meant that they were entirely dependent on the Sulpician priests, who never swore vows of poverty, to keep the land in “trust” for them. Instead, the Sulpicians sold 98% of the land and set up the deeply unequal and racist structures at the core of conflicts today.

There has never been an adequate response to right the historical wrongs from the Government nor the settler public.
However, in their land registries the Sulpicians painstakingly recorded every property transaction they made with settlers for more than 200 years. By reconstructing the historical cadaster of the Seigneurie du Lac des Deux Montagnes and locating the initial deeds of sale and concession for each individual lot, we can analyze and understand the rhythm, geographical distribution, and spatial dynamics of these transactions. This information provides valuable insights into the colonization process and its significant impact on the people of Kanehsatà:ke.
